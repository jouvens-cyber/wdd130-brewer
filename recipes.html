The Signature Dill Recipe

The Signature Dill Recipe

This classic, everyday garlic dill recipe makes two quart-sized jars of crisp refrigerator pickles.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs firm pickling cucumbers
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups white distilled vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 6 cloves garlic, peeled and lightly smashed
  • 1 large bunch of fresh dill
  • 2 teaspoons whole mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns

Instructions

  1. Prep the Cucumbers: Wash the cucumbers thoroughly in cold water. Trim away a small slice from the blossom end of each cucumber. Leave them whole for the signature pickle experience.
  2. Make the Brine: In a medium saucepan, combine the water, white vinegar, and kosher salt. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Stir until the salt is dissolved. Remove from the heat and let it cool for about 5 minutes.
  3. Pack the Jars: Divide the garlic, fresh dill, mustard seeds, and black peppercorns between two clean quart-sized glass jars. Pack the cucumbers tightly into the jars.
  4. Pour the Brine: Carefully pour the warm brine over the cucumbers until they are covered. Leave about half an inch of empty space at the top of each jar.
  5. Cool and Store: Let the jars sit uncovered at room temperature until the glass and liquid cool completely. Put the lids on tightly and move the jars to the refrigerator.
  6. Cure: Let the pickles rest in the refrigerator for at least 48 hours before opening. They can stay in the refrigerator for up to two months.
